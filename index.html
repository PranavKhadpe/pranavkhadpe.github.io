I am a 5th year undergraduate student pursuing a B.Tech and M.Tech in the Department of Electrical Engineering at IIT Kharagpur. I pursue interdisciplinary research with a thrust towards Human Computer Interaction and Social Computing. At IIT Kharagpur, I have had the pleasure of working with Prof. Plaban Bhowmick, Prof. Pawan Goyal and Prof. Ashish Ranjan Hota. I have previously interned with the HCI group at Stanford University working with Prof. Michael Bernstein and Ranjay Krishna and Microsoft Research India where I worked with Dr. Monojit Choudhury and others in the Multilingual Systems Group.

Information technologies have revolutionized the way we learn, work, and interact. Having relied on these systems to learn and grow, myself, I am passionate about enabling others to do so and to do so effectively. To this end, I am interested in building systems that make it easier for people to learn, work, and access information. In order to inform the design of novel systems, I am also keen on studying how people communicate and behave while engaging in these processes. Parallely, I am interested in moving beyond systems that work for some people in some contexts and building systems that more people can derive utility from, informed by an understanding of users' preferences, capacities, and resource constraints in different contexts.
